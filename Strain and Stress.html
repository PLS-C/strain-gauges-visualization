Material Properties Guide

  • E — Young's modulus (GPa)

    This number shows how stiff the material is. A high E means the bar is hard to stretch.

  • ν — Poisson's ratio

    When you pull the bar longer, it also gets thinner. ν tells how much thinner it gets.

  • GF — Gauge factor

    This number shows how much the wire resistance changes when the bar stretches. A bigger GF means a bigger change.

  • ρ — Resistivity (Ω·m)

    This shows how hard it is for electricity to flow through the material. High ρ means more resistance.

  • α — Temperature coefficient (1/°C)

    When the temperature changes, the resistance changes too. α tells how much it changes for each degree.

  • σy — Yield strength (MPa)

    This is the biggest stress the material can take before it starts to bend and does not go back.
